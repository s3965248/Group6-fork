Detailed Description - Plans and Progress

The story of our project:

How it began:

The idea for our project was first thought of by one of our team members (Anna) for the assignment project idea section of Assignment 1. Anna had this idea because she enjoys growing house plants and various succulents but struggled with the cost of buying plants from nurseries as well as the difficulty of finding plants in local nurseries or having to get them shipped. She knew that many plants are easy to propagate and frequently gave away cuttings to her friends, so that they could grow their own. This gave her the idea to provide likeminded people with a platform to trade plants, seedlings and propagations to benefit everyone involved.

How it has progressed:

Through assignments 2 and 3 we have been able to expand on exactly what we want our application to do (functionality) and how we plan on doing it. We’ve also divided the functionality of our application into 2 releases as well as determining how we want it look (design/colour scheme). We have been able to do more in depth research on the tools, technologies, and skills needed to create the application.

What the project will do:

Our project will provide people with an interest in growing plants with a platform to trade cuttings and seedlings with likeminded people in their local communities. This will save people money as well as providing people with a chance to meet people locally with the same interests. We hope to create an application that is easy to use while keeping people’s private information safe./

The key features we plan on having done by the submission deadline are:

  • A Login page – A page for the user to sign in using their e-mail address and password or alternatively sign up using the sign-up page by entering an e-mail address, username, and password.
  • A Pofile page – Our plan is for this include a few details about the user as well as a list of the plants they have available.
  • A form where users can list plants with tags – This page will allow the user to enter the details of each individual plant they have available, and it will allow them to add tags (location and plant-type) to make them easier for other users to find.
  • Listings page – This page should provide a user with a list of plants available near their location. The user can choose to contact other users from the listings to enquire about a plant.
  • Database to populate this info to Listings page – We plan to incorporate a database to store the information added by users through the form to be displayed in the listings page for all users to see.
  • A working app from this created on React Native – we plan to use React Native to make the app useable on all platforms.

How we will do it:

We plan to have two members of our team take charge of the deliverable portion of our project (the app prototype) with the rest of the members helping with smaller parts where needed and creating the plan and wireframes etc. We have decided to assign Jordan as the backend developer which in the case of our current project means creating the database required for the listings page of our application, which he plans to do using the programming language Java. Matthew has been assigned the role of the frontend developer for our project. This includes creating the design and format of the application which they plan to do using HTML and CSS. Anna has been assigned the role of UI/UX designer which mainly involves her creating the wireframe for our project (to create a visual idea to show to testers as well as to help Matthew in the actual creation of the app).

Our plan consists of 6 stages:

  • Stage 1 (written parts of report): The first stage is one of the most important stages as it will shape what we will be doing in every other stage and how we will do it. We will decide and plan exactly what we believe we can accomplish in the limited time frame to get the best result possible. This stage will consist of writing the report which will mainly consist of the description of our project.
  • Stage 2 (research/website creation): In this stage each member of our team will research programs/coding languages/frameworks/etc. they will need to know to get their tasks done. In this stage the initial pages and structure of our website will also be created and the design and look of the website will begin to take shape.
  • Stage 3 (app creation – login page): Stage 3 of our plan will focus on creating the sign-in/sign-up page of our app.
  • Stage 4 (app creation – profile page): Stage 4 of our plan will focus on creating the profile page of our app.
  • Stage 5 (app creation – listings page/database): Stage 5 of our plan will focus on creating the listings page which will also include a form to add listings to the page. This stage of the plan also includes creating a database to display this information and will therefore involve the incorporation of the database into the project.
  • Stage 6 (app creation and testing – React Native): In this stage of our plan our app should be finished and ready for React Native to be added and tested. Testing will be done using the app and the wireframe which will include the entire release 1 functionality.

Changes made to the project plan:

  • We had originally hope to use React Native to turn Plantr into an application native to both IOS and Android. Unfortunately, we’ve found that with the limited time we given to create the deliverables for this assignment we will be unable to incorporate React Native into our project.
  • Due to in part Jordans brief absence because of contracting COVID 19 and his struggle to get the backend working, Matthew also took on an attempt at the backend. Jordan was working on creating the backend using Java while Matthew was attempting it using MySQL and JavaScript.
  • In the last week of the project, we ended up using Jordan’s backend program and having Matthew create the frontend (back to the original plan). Unfortunately, the functional code will not work on the GitHub page as GitHub won’t host dynamic websites. However, the functional app will be displayed in the presentation video for assignment 5.
