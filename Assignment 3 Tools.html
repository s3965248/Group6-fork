GitHub:

A fork of the GitHub repository we used in Assessment 2 was used to create our website for Assessment 3. Separate pages were created to make both assessments accessible from the same website. Atom was used with GitHub Desktop to create, edit, and add all the parts of our website to the GitHub repository. The new design aspects and formatting were created by team member Marah while the rest of the team members added and organised the written parts of the report that they were each assigned. For this assessment the involvement of most members of the group to the repository was minimal as they were involved more in other parts of the assignment.

The individual contribution graphs for this Assessment reflect the actual contributions of our group’s members incredibly poorly as most work was done using different tools (discussed in the ‘Tools and Technologies’ section of the report). Most of the team are shown to have only contributed toward the end of the timeline however, in the meantime, Anna was working on creating the wireframe, storyboard, and editing the presentation video, Jordan was working on creating the backend of the PlantR application, Matthew was working on creating the frontend of the PlantR application, and Shannon was writing the script and filming the presentation content, as well as everyone working on writing the content for the report.

Graph of overall contributions to repository:
Although there was much more work done away from the repository for this assessment (using different tools for different parts) than the previous one, this graph still reflects our efforts, from the beginning to the end of the time spent on the assessment, quite well. It shows that we had a strong start from the beginning on July 20th, then a dip while tasks were being split between group members and the content of the report was being written and edited, and then a strong finish until the deadline we set for the website to be finished on August 17th. The rising contributions show the effort put in by Marah to improve the design aspects of our group’s website and the peak at the end of the timeline shows all the team members adding their parts to the website after everything had been written, proofread, and edited.

Anna: Anna’s graph shows her adding her work to the repository towards the end of the timeline with added effort in coming back and ensuring that any flaws were fixed. She also added a page for the presentation to be displayed on our group’s website.

Jordan: Jordan’s graph reflects that most of his work was contributions to backend of the application used as our deliverable rather than the GitHub repository. His contribution to the website was simply adding the parts that he wrote which includes the ‘scopes and limits’ and ‘group processes’ sections.

Shannon: Unfortunately, Shannon’s graph is also deceiving as it only portrays them adding their content at the end of the timeline. But Shannon did a lot of the background work, on top of filming the presentation, for this assessment, they proofread and edited a lot of the content for the report, they wrote the script for the presentation and facilitated communication between group members that were having issues.

Matthew: Matthew’s graph does not reflect their efforts toward this assessment at all. According to the graph, you would think they made no contributions. However, Matthew was putting in constant effort to creating the frontend of the application and even the backend when Jordan was suddenly ill.

Marah: Marah was the main contributor to the GitHub repository as she was assigned the task of enhancing the design and look of the group’s website. From the beginning of the timeline to the end, she was experimenting with different effects and design ideas that could be added to make the website look a little more professional than it did for the previous assessment. The graph reflects the added effort she put in from the middle of the timeline to the end, when she finished the multiple written sections assigned to her and focused more on improving the website.

Microsoft Teams:

Again, Microsoft Teams was used as our team’s base for communication. However, this time we decided to split up the different areas of work into separate channels for added organisation and clarity. We created 4 new channels; ‘A3 Meetings, Scheduling’ is the channel where meetings were held and any concerns regarding scheduling were discussed, ‘A3 Project (App Development) is where the deliverables were discussed including the wireframe and testing forms, ‘A3 Written Report and Website’ was used for discussing website developments and the written sections that needed to be included in the report document, and lastly ‘A5 Presentation’ is where all aspects of the presentation for Assessment 5 were discussed. Biweekly meetings were also held to assign work and to check in on progress as well as to brainstorm ideas for website design, deliverables, and the presentation.
