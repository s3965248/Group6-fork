What is it?

Cyber security is the defence against cyber threats for systems connected to the internet, including their hardware, software, and data. Individuals and businesses both utilise this to prevent illegal access to data centres and other digital systems. These cyber attacks typically try to gain access to, alter, or delete sensitive data; demand money from users; or obstruct regular corporate operations.

What is new in cyber security?

Growing threat of ransomware

Ransomware has become more popular over the past 20 years because it can more easily bring financial rewards to hackers. Ransomware is currently thought to come in 124 different families, and hackers are already quite skilled at concealing dangerous code. The most recent and advanced malware is not always necessary for hackers to succeed; it is not too difficult for a hacker to do. Nearly a quarter of the incident-response engagements for IBM Security’s X-Force threat intelligence group last year involved ransomware.

The internet of vulnerable things (The Internet of Things evolving)

This year, the number of interconnected devices is expected to reach 18 billion. As a result, there are a lot more potential access points for cyber criminals trying to break into secure digital systems. The Internet of Things (IoT) has long been recognised as posing a specific threat. Past attacks have included hackers using connected home appliances like fridges and kettles to gain access to networks, then using those connections to gain access to computers or phones where valuable data may be stored.

Remote working cyber security risks

One of the most talked about trends in cyber security is working from home, which presents new cyber security threats. Compared to centralised offices, which typically have more secure routers, firewalls, and access control systems managed by IT security teams, home offices are much less safe. Traditional security vetting may not have been as thorough as usual in the race to keep things operating. As a result, cybercriminals may have adapted their strategies to their benefit. In addition to having mobile apps for instant messaging services like Microsoft Teams and Zoom, many employees use their own devices for two-factor authentication. The possibility that private information could end up in the wrong hands grows because of these fuzzier distinctions between personal and professional life. As a result, a crucial trend in cyber security is for businesses to concentrate on the security issues associated with remote workforces.

What can be done now?

AI-powered cyber security

Human beings are unable to handle the volume of cyber security threats on their own. As a result, businesses are increasingly using machine learning and artificial intelligence to improve their security infrastructure. There are also financial benefits to doing this; in 2020, firms who experienced a data breach but had fully implemented AI (Artificial Intelligence) technology saved an average of $3.58 million.

Natural language processing, face recognition, and autonomous security systems have all benefited greatly from AI. AI also allows for far faster analysis of vast amounts of risk data. Both large businesses dealing with large volumes of data and small or mid-sized businesses with under-resourced security teams can benefit from this.

Multi-factor authentication improving

Multi-factor authentication is regarded as the gold standard of authentication(“Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is Regarded as the Gold ... - IT Voice”). However, malicious actors are developing new strategies to get around it, particularly when authentication is done by SMS or phone calls. Because of this, Microsoft suggested that customers discontinue using phone-based MFA (Multi Factor Authentication) in 2020 and switch to app-based authenticators and security keys. Although SMS offers some built-in security, the messages delivered, including those used for authentication, are not encrypted. Because of this, people are now capable of automating man-in-the-middle attacks to collect plain-text one-time passcodes. This creates vulnerability for actions like online banking, where SMS is frequently used for authentication.

What is likely to be able to be done soon (say in the next 3 years)?

By 2025, 80% of enterprises will adopt a strategy to unify web, cloud services and private application access from a single vendor’s SSE platform(“Gartner: 8 Ways Cybersecurity Will Change Companies”) . Companies are providing an integrated security service edge (SSE) solution to expedite and privatise online access to their customers together with Security as a Service (SaaS) application security as hybrid and remote work continue to gain popularity and regularity. The best efficiency and security effectiveness in this area can be achieved by switching to a single-vendor system.

By 2025, threat actors will have weaponised operational technology environments successfully to cause human casualties. Unfortunately, as IoT spreads throughout big cities, these gadgets become more vulnerable to online assaults. The potential for real-world threats to people and the environment are increased by the capacity of hackers to access equipment like streetlights, creating chances for criminals to exploit these linked devices.

By 2025, 70% of CEOs will mandate a culture of organisational resilience to survive coinciding threats from cybercrime, sever weather events, civil unrest, and political instabilities. The COVID-19 pandemic exposed numerous companies’ shortcomings in the case of a significant disruption. Within the next three years, organisations are expected to have increased planning and support in place because of the pandemic, making organisational resilience a primary priority going forwards.

What is the likely impact?

What is the potential impact of this development?

Cyber security has always been an uphill battle, but change is happening faster now. Businesses continue to make investments in technology to run their operations. To support remote work, improve the customer experience, and generate revenue, they are currently adding more technologies to their IT networks, which could lead to new vulnerabilities.

The traditional threat of a lone hacker is no longer present. Cyber hacking is a multibillion-dollar industry nowadays, they have institutional structures and R&D budgets. Attackers employ innovative tools like automation, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. They will be able to shorten the end-to-end attack life cycle, from reconnaissance to exploitation, from weeks to days or hours during the next few years.

Cyber risk management has not kept pace with the proliferation of digital analytics transformations, and many companies are not sure how to identify and manage digital risks.

Which people will be most affected and how?

New threats surface every hour of every day in the technological world of today. By connecting to the internet, the likelihood that a hacker may target your company increases. Cybercrime danger and cybercrime are major global concerns for businesses and governments. If organisations do not have a suitable cyber security plan, there are significant financial and reputational consequences.

Will this create, replace, or make redundant any current jobs or technologies?

The increase in cybercrime risk that comes with the development of technology will result in an increase in jobs for cyber security professionals. Globally, cybercrime is increasing, a corresponding rise in demand for cyber security professionals has created a skill shortage, pushing up salaries. Future career growth in cyber security is anticipated to increase tremendously.

How will this affect you?

Cyber security affects everyone in their daily lives in this technology dependent time. Most people use the internet every single day of their lives nowadays. Without cyber security anyone could get a hold of our privileged information anytime they wanted to. The risk of being targeted by cyber criminals is much lower for individuals than it is for businesses, governments, and corporations but, it is still important for the internet to be safe for everyone. Electrical blackouts, equipment failure, and disclosure of sensitive national security information can all be brought on by cyberattacks. They may lead to the theft of priceless and confidential information, including medical records. They may paralyse systems or interfere with phone and computer networks, rendering data inaccessible. Cyber security can affect anyone whether it be directly right from the start or after some time. It is not an exaggeration to say that cyber threats may affect the functioning of life as we know it.

There are many types of cyber attacks that can affect both individuals and bigger companies. Phishing is a cyber attack carried out via email in which a recipient is duped into opening a link and downloading malicious software or divulging personal information. Ransomware is a cyber attack that encrypts data on the target machine and holds the user hostage until they pay a ransom to unlock the data.

Cyber security development affects you, your friends, and your family in all these ways. Any of these things could happen to you, your friends, or your family or even businesses or governments that will eventually have an effect on you indirectly.
